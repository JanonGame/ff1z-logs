
# The Returner


A voice carries up from the lower level. It takes a moment to place it.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Doesn't that sound like... Tiz?"

> [!dlg-guiscard-r] Guiscard
> "Dear lord, I hope he doesn't think he's getting paid for coming all the way out here."

![[Chaos_Shrine_Temple_Outside.png]]
> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "Thank goodness it's you three.
>
> I uh... I thought I'd give it another try."

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "You actually decided to take us up on that offer?"

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "It didn't seem right to sit around feeling sorry for myself for so long.
>
> Elle's gonna hate me for this when I get back, but...
>
> So I decided to come and help."

He does not have his old sword. He has a small gladius instead. He pulls it out as they find him a way up, brandishing it with shaky determination.

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "I might not have my old sword, but this will have to do."

Silas greets him with all the warmth of a man who has just been told he has new paperwork.

> [!dlg-silas-r] Silas
> "And who is this guy? Another straggler?"

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Someone with a score to settle. That's all that matters."

Brigid narrows her eyes at Tiz as he rubs his arm. She doesn't say anything. She nods, and points at the tower.

The group presses on. As the stairs climb higher, Silas mentions he is feeling nauseous. The draft grows colder with every floor. The party notes the pattern: rooms housing orbs representing the four elements. Audra confirms she felt a powerful wind blocking her approach to the tower on her first attempt.

---
# The Scientific Method

Looking for it, they find a room with a wind orb and a set of bones on the floor. Iomene quietly lines the bones up properly and says a small prayer for whoever they belonged to. The orb radiates necrotic energy along with the wind. The party's investigation goes poorly enough that Luthien encourages Altan to keep poking at it while the rest of them stand back, for science.

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "Ah, so you're encouraging me to die this time, huh?
>
> Well, I always knew I'd go out like this some day."

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "I'm merely joking. It's worth noting there's some necrotic energy emanating from those bones as well."

Luthien fires at the orb. It releases a blast of wind that throws most of the room back but does not connect with the orb. She fires again. This time it connects. 

> [!narration]
> Emerald Fragment acquired!

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "As usual, the scientific method leads to favorable results!"

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "I don't remember too many scholars who died and *then* achieved what they were trying to, so let's keep this science to a minimum."

---

# Blast from the Past

They continue upward. On the next landing, Guiscard spots the skeletons a fraction of a second before they move. The fight is a messy one. There are more of them than expected and they hit harder than their condition implies. One gets hold of Guiscard.

> [!dlg-guiscard-r] Guiscard
> "Mon dieu, he's got ahold of me!"
>
> "I've disarmed him! Allons-y!"

Audra takes a critical hit and goes down briefly. Beck wards the room against undead and lends what healing he can. Tiz, making his first real contribution, lands clean hits on the remaining skeletons. The orb powering them is dealt with shortly after.

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Even with no skeletons remaining, we should take down the orb, yes?"

Guiscard tells Beck to tend to Audra, that he is fine.

> [!dlg-audra-r] Audra
> "You certainly handled them well, Guiscard. You have our respect."

> [!dlg-silas] Silas
> "Hmph. For now, at least."

> [!dlg-beck-r] Beck
> "This isn't the time for petty sniping, Silas. Not when we're close."


> [!narration]
> Topaz Fragment acquired!

Guiscard asks if that was the complex problem-solving they teach in wizard college. Nobody answers. Tiz lingers near one of the murals on the wall, staring at it.

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "Mon dieu, Tiz, don't look at that. That's not good for you. Find another mural, there were some less horrific ones in the other room."

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "It just reminded me of something I was told once."

The mural depicts a lich commanding the risen dead to turn against their own. Tiz recounts the story as he knows it: two thousand years ago, a demon called **Chaos** entered the human world, bringing with him four archfiends. The **Warriors of Light** came, defeated Chaos, and sealed him in this shrine. The story has been kept alive by the royal family and passed down through the Returners as a kind of founding myth. Most people have forgotten it entirely by now.

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "Something tells me the story won't be the only thing still alive soon enough."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Most old tales get warped over time, and 2000 years is not a trivial span. I'd wager the only reliable record belongs to the royal family."

Brigid cuts the discussion short. She wants to press forward while they still have the chance.

> [!dlg-brigid-r] Brigid
> "I'd just like to put this to rest. That's all."

---
Altan opens a door he perhaps should not have, and a **Tonberry** is on the other side of it. Iomene shoves a large armoire against the door while the Tonberry stabs at it repeatedly and futilely from the other side. When the thumping stops, everyone breathes out.

> [!dlg-beck] Beck
> "It seems like we're fine."

They can feel it now, as they climb the final stairs. The same charge as the other orb rooms, but stronger. The whole tower feels like it is humming.

> [!dlg-iomene-r] Iomene
> "Oh, I sometimes get this feeling when touching a handrail on an escalator."

They enter the last floor.

---

# Bad Knight? Good Night for you

![[Chaos_Shrine_Last_Floor.png]]

Princess Sarah is already there, her voice tight with desperation. Garland stands over the statues, the ritual almost complete.

> [!dlg-sarah] Princess Sarah
> "Jack, please stop this! How could you do this to your own people?
>
> You're just a devil wearing Jack's face."

> [!dlg-garland-r] Garland
> "A devil, you say? You don't know the half of it.
>
> But when I become complete, and destroy your precious kingdom, you can see just how much of a devil I can be."

The party enters. Garland turns and looks them over with something between contempt and amusement.

> [!dlg-garland-r] Garland
> "Ahh. My dear subordinates came to be my undoing, have you?"

Brigid has an arrow nocked. Her hand is shaking.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Cease this foolishness, Garland. What happened to you? What is all of this about?"

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "What motivation drove you to throw away all your honor and renown?"

> [!dlg-garland-r] Garland
> "With this, I am to become all-powerful.
>
> With this, I am to become Chaos."

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "All-powerful? Garland, Chaos was *banished.* Does that sound like the kind of power you want?"

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "All-powerful in a world where power won't matter. You don't have to throw everything away, Garland."

> [!dlg-garland-r] Garland
> "I told you. I am no mere servant of Chaos.
>
> I am to *become* Chaos.
>
> And I'll kill anyone who tries to oppose me."

Luthien gets one more question in before he moves.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "When did you first hear *his* words?"

Garland flinches. Then the fight begins.

![[GarlandThrone.png]]

Guiscard passes the Black Crystal to Altan as the party spreads out. Iomene lights Garland up with magic, outlining him in violet so no one can miss their mark. Audra enters her Dragoon's Trance, leaps thirty feet into the air, and comes down hard. Tiz fights his way through the fear, succeeds, and lands two solid hits, which is when Garland finally looks at him and recognizes him.

> [!dlg-garland-r] Garland
> "You were that pathetic knight from the castle, weren't you?
>
> I'll kill you and let you join your comrades."

Garland draws a hidden handle from his sword. The blade splits into two: a long sword in his right hand, a shorter blade in his left. A deep red aura rolls off him. Those with sharp enough eyes see something flash behind him for just a moment: the image of a serpentine woman wielding six swords.

Garland reaches up toward the black orb. Black lightning arcs from it into his hand and then into four of the companions, Brigid and Silas among them. The fight gets worse before it gets better.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "What's wrong, Garland? I thought you were going to show us a thing or two."

Tiz, after taking a hit and struggling with his emotions, fights through it and strikes twice more. Sarah, still in the room, acts on her own, calling out and breaking Garland's concentration at a critical moment.

Guiscard lands the final blow. 

> [!narration]
> Garland goes down.

> [!dlg-guiscard-r] Guiscard
> "Alright, the beast is down. Doctors, do your thing. I've got to go pass out after that one. I'm taking the sword home, wrap it for me."

The room is quiet. Brigid, panting, checks that Audra is unhurt. *Good.*
Brigid unsheathes a knife, and staggers toward Garland's unconscious body.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Hold it. That man knows more than he let on here. The threat isn't over yet. He needs to face the king's justice."

> [!dlg-brigid-r] Brigid
> "He killed Mia's best friend."

> [!dlg-sarah] Princess Sarah
> "Please, Brigid, don't kill him. He's..."

> [!dlg-brigid-r] Brigid
> "Move."

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "He'll get what's coming his way either way. But none of us arrived here to execute an unconscious man. And bloodying your own hands will only turn one evil into two."

Brigid stares at her for a long moment.

> [!dlg-brigid-r] Brigid
> "Fine.
>
> You all can have it how you want."

Audra takes her aside. The rest of the group turns to Sarah.

> [!dlg-sarah] Princess Sarah
> "Thank you for coming all this way, just to save me.
>
> His eyes were always so gentle, like the midday sky. But after he returned from the Chaos Shrine, it was like night and day.
>
> I tried to tend to his wounds, but something about my healing magic made him recoil. He struck at my hand and wouldn't let me near him."

Altan brings up the Black Crystal, the egg found in the vine-covered hallway. Sarah goes still.

> [!dlg-sarah] Princess Sarah
> "When Garland found me near that room, he was so angry. He said he would kill me if I ever tried to take it."

The theory forming among the group is that the crystal holds whatever remained of the real Garland, sealed away to make room for whatever Chaos put in its place. Sarah asks to keep it. Guiscard, watching her carefully, hands it over without argument.

> [!dlg-sarah] Princess Sarah
> "I will let mother and father know about this.
>
> ...I just worry for what will become of Garland, even if he were to wake up. What people will say. What they might do."

> [!dlg-beck-r] Beck
> "Even if it is true that Garland was being controlled...no amount of explanation will bring Zest back."

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "...I know.
>
> But if it were true that Garland was being controlled to do all the things he did...
>
> I want to find some way to, some day, forgive him for it."

> [!dlg-beck-r] Beck
> "...I think I want to as well.
>
> As hard as that sounds."
