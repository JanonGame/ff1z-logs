# Guiscard works his charm

The necklace does its job at the castle gates. The guards recognize it immediately, and the party is waved through without much argument. Inside, they run into Beck and Silas again.

Beck mentions that the king's strategy meeting is wrapping up and there is talk of sending a guerilla unit north to the Chaos Shrine. He steps away briefly to fetch someone, leaving the party with Silas.

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "So Silas, how's the wife?"

> [!dlg-silas-r] Silas 
> "Is that meant to be a jest of sorts?"

While Guiscard cheerfully works on Silas, he spots two figures moving through the corridor and excuses himself to intercept them. One is Princess Mia. The other, a black-haired girl who introduces herself curtly as Brigid, does not take her eyes off him. Guiscard presents himself as having been brought in for the investigation and asks what they know about Garland's state of mind in the days before the shrine.

> [!dlg-mia] Princess Mia
> "Garland was always so nice. My sister... I hope she's okay. She and he got along so well because of how gentle he was. But I remember when he came back from the ruins, there was something really different. I heard him shout at Sarah. He had never done something like that before."

> [!dlg-brigid-r] Brigid
> "I think it's clear he wanted something from her."

Guiscard presses gently on what that something might be. 

> [!dlg-mia] Princess Mia
> "I think that Garland wanted to take the power we have from her.
>
> Father says the Chaos Shrine was where the Warriors of Light entombed Chaos a long time ago. I think he plans to break the seal. But why would he do that?"

She thanks him for his concern and understanding, and says that they'd really appreciate it if he could bring Garland to his senses. 

> [!dlg-mia] Princess Mia

> [!narration]
> *"How...DARE YOU!!!"*

Before she can finish the thought, Silas has already started making a scene.

# The Throne Room
Beck opens the throne room door and waves them in. Guiscard leads, spine straight, head high, confidently introducing the group. Iomene quickly checks that her uniform and hair are in order before following. Luthien boldly announces their intent to pursue Garland to his hideout and rescue his hostage. Altan slides in quietly behind everyone else, as if he had been there the whole time, and bows.

The room holds Beck, Silas, Audra, Brigid, and a bearded man with sharp eyes who takes one look at the two elves and directs a distinctly distrusting glare their way. Luthien glares straight back. Iomene settles the matter by grabbing Luthien's skull in what, to an outside observer, looks exactly like a headpat.

The bearded man, **Minister Lagone**, leans down to whisper something to the king. King Cornelia's eyebrows go up. He stands, steps down from the throne, and addresses the group directly.

> [!dlg-cornelia] King Cornelia 
> "It seems that Minister Lagone knows of the... exploits of the noble family of Dervin.
> 
> It would be dishonest of me to say I do not have my reservations on the matter.
> 
> And yet, with my daughter having been taken to that horrid place, the Chaos Shrine, we have no word of how she fares even now. So, in spite of everything, I am willing to put aside my reservations, in the hopes that you will rescue her."

Silas looks genuinely surprised. Beck allows himself a relieved smile.

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien 
> "Very kind of you, Your Majesty. You have my word that we will not disappoint."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene 
> "I thank you for looking past certain... imperfect records."





Minister Lagone, having made his reservations known, steps aside and lets Beck and Audra lay out the plan. The army will move along the coast and through the mountain pass to surround the shrine and deal with the monsters outside. Beck, Silas, Brigid, and Audra were tasked with slipping in through the front and handling what's inside. With the party along, their numbers are effectively doubled.

> [!dlg-brigid] Brigid
> "I wouldn't be so sure about that."

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "She's right. It might even be effectively tripled."

Guiscard floats the idea of a siege to starve out the shrine's forces. Luthien and Iomene both point out, at the same time, that the princess's survival makes that a non-starter. 

Audra confirms she has done reconnaissance on the shrine as a member of the Flying Drakes. She could not get an interior view, as the entrance is sealed by magic only a royal key can open, but she managed to narrow down where Garland is gathering his power: the highest tower of the Chaos Shrine, where he appears to be drawing from a set of statues. Before she could get closer, a Zu drove her off. It seems to be guarding the tower entrance specifically.

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Oh, I'm somewhat familiar with statues. Sculpting is a hobby of mine."

> [!dlg-audra-r] Audra
> "Oh..."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "I likely won't be able to directly deal with the magic inside them, but I can perhaps offer insight on who built them and for what purpose."

The question of whether the statues can simply be smashed comes up. Brigid does not particularly care either way so long as they stop feeding Garland's magic. The question of whether the Zu is intelligent earns a dry response from Brigid: it is more intelligent than any bird she has hunted before. The meeting wraps up. Beck tells them to be ready at dawn.

> [!dlg-cornelia] King Cornelia
> "May the light of the crystals be with you."
