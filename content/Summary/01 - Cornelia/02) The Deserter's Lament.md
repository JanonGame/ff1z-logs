# Public Enemy Number Two

They make their way through the city streets, with Luthien checking around for info on where Tiz lives.
> [!dlg-npc-r] Passerby
> "Tiz, you say?
>
> ......
>
> Ah, that cowardly man. 
> 
> Yes, I've seen his residence. It's down that way. 
> 
> Trust me, you won't miss it."

The directions prove accurate. The house is not hard to find. The words tacked to its side make sure of that.

> [!narration]
> *Coward. Deserter. Onion Knight.*
>
> *Die.*
>

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "Seems like a beloved pillar of the community."

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "See, if I had this reputation I'd only get two bags of anthrax in my mailbox each month instead of three."

Guiscard rips the papers off and knocks politely. The woman who answers the door looks tired in a way that goes beyond a rough week. Dark circles, clothes slightly dirtied, blinds drawn in every room behind her. She opens with *if this is a prank, I'd appreciate if you'd--* and stops when she notices Iomene's uniform.

She introduces herself as **Elle**, Tiz's fiancee. She makes tea, both Mimett Greens and Sylkis Greens, and listens. Tiz, she says, has barely been able to get out of bed since the night of the siege. He used to have such a spark. She does not know what to do. Iomene asks if there is anything she can do to help.

> [!dlg-elle] Elle
> "My Tiz is upstairs, in our room. I only ask that you try not to startle him. As you've probably seen, we've been getting very aggressive guests these days."

---

# Running to Fight Another Day

Iomene and Guiscard head upstairs. Tiz is sitting at the bedside with a book, not facing the door. He is unkempt in the way of someone who has stopped noticing. He puts the book down when he hears them come in.

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "I'm sorry, I didn't realize we had guests."

The book is by Wedge Cunningway. Iomene mentions her husband works with Wedge regularly.

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "Really...is he really as weird as they make him out to be on TV?"

It breaks the ice. Tiz admits he has been thinking about moving to Onrac with Elle. A fresh start. Then his head hangs, and the party understands that he sees this fresh start as running away. Again.

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "I have a feeling there is something else influencing your decision."

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "...I take it at least one of you knows all about me. Otherwise you probably wouldn't have wasted your time coming all this way just to talk about Wedge Cunningway."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "So far I've only heard the story out on the streets, and as a metropolitan I know how quickly the truth gets warped into something unrecognizable.
>
> ...Do you feel comfortable talking about what happened on that fateful night?"

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "Honestly? No.
>
> No, I don't...
>
> But I can say I appreciate you coming to ask me about the truth of what happened that day, instead of what's been happening with me and my darling Elle.
>
> ...So I'll try my best to explain."

Luthien, who has been letting Iomene do the talking, steps in.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Mister Tiz, your words may be able to save lives and lead to some closure for you as well. 
> 
> If you need time, we understand. 
> 
> But, please do know the following:
> 
> You deserve to be heard as well."

Tiz collects himself and starts from the beginning. The monster attacks on the outer wall had been increasing in frequency, each wave coming every other day. Garland concluded something was wrong at the northern shrine and went to investigate alone. The sky looked terrible that night, the kind of terrible that made people assume he had died up there. When he came back the next day, the relief in the city was genuine.

But that night, Tiz was on duty as usual, and they found **Zest** dead in his bed.

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "Jack Garland's not just any ordinary man. Not anymore.
>
> I thought that maybe, maybe I wasn't cut out for this.
> 
> I don't know whose responsibility it is to stop him now... "

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Ours."

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "What?
> 
> You're not seriously thinking about going out there to find him, are you?"

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "We're not thinking about it. 
> 
> We ***are*** going after him."

> [!dlg-iomene-r] Iomene
> "Hmm, despite what I may have implied earlier, your bluntness really is one of your charming points."

Guiscard asks about Garland's behavior in the days before the shrine, whether anything seemed off. Tiz mentions that Silas could speak to it more directly, but that even he noticed something in a spar between Garland and Silas that felt like it nearly went too far. He nearly killed Silas.

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "The only reason I joined the Returners was for my best friend. And when I squared up with Jack Garland of all people, and Zest died, I realized how out of my depth I was."

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Tiz, it wasn't your fault. If anything, you should be commended for stepping out of your depth."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "On that night and against that man in particular, you were not being your usual self."

Tiz doesn't say anything for a moment. He gets up and goes to a nearby shelf.

He hands them two Potions of Healing, and then a necklace.

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "This belonged to Zest. He said he wanted me to have it if I ever needed it, but I let him down by not avenging him that night.
>
> I want you to have it. Do what I couldn't.
>
> I'm sure if you have this, someone will let you in the castle for sure. He was a loved man among the castle residents, after all."

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "Thank you Tiz. We'll make sure we do right by Zest. This won't happen again."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Your information and aid we gratefully accept. I know I may sound presumptuous, but if you recover, you will always be welcome to join us."

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "Maybe.
>
> ...Thanks. For listening, and everything."

Elle thanks them warmly on the way out and tells them they are welcome back any time.
Luthien pauses at the door and turns back to add one last thing.

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
>"Sometimes... it pays to fight another day. It doesn't make you weak. It makes you a fighter."

He doesn't respond. He doesn't need to.
