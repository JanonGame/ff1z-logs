# Death from Above
They barely have time to get their bearings before the sky opens up. Giant birds, big enough to blot out the sun for a moment, come screaming over the rooftops dropping dire wolves directly into the crowded plaza. The Returners militia scrambles. The party draws steel.
One of the wolves goes straight for Iomene. It gets its teeth into her before anyone can stop it, which is when Luthien comes tearing across the plaza screaming at it to get its filthy fangs off her. She hits it with her blade, Altan deploys some impressive magics, and Iomene, apparently unbothered by being actively mauled, draws her gunblade and starts putting in the real work. 
Meanwhile a stranger slips through the chaos, materializes behind the second wolf, and buries a dagger in it with calm efficiency.

Both wolves are down in under a minute. The stranger is already wiping his dagger on a handkerchief by the time the dust settles.

# The Smith's Advice

The group heads to Jonah's blacksmithery at the behest of Guiscard. A reliable business partner of his. He goes in first to discuss some affairs with Jonah, and returns quickly to allow them inside.
Guiscard waves the party in and gives them the brief: same story they gave him, Jonah will ask questions to make sure they are worth trusting.

> "I'm Jonah Levenson, and this is my smithery. If you ever are in need of some *fine* metalwork, this is the place for it!
> 
> First order of business, you have my name, of course. I'm hoping I can get yours as well."

Help Cornelia deal with its problem, he suggests, and Cornelia might be in a position to help with theirs. Guiscard puts it more bluntly: he and Jonah have a vested interest in the city's safety, and after sinking a lot of money into getting Jonah a new forge, he cannot afford to have terrorist attacks carried out in the city.

Guiscard launches into a full sales pitch about its ergonomic hilt and how it lets swordfighters with waning wrist strength fight like a twenty-four year old at the top of his game, 1400 gil plus tax. Iomene informs them she left her wallet at home. Jonah's smile deflates slightly.

The lead they do have is a man named **Tiz**, a soldier who was present the night of the siege and laid down his arms. Guiscard thinks he will be easier to approach than most, but that they will need to be delicate about it.

> [!dlg-guiscard-r] Guiscard
