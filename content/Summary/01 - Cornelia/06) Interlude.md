# Clocking Out for the Day

The royal party is a few days away. A proper gala, thrown in celebration of the princess's safe return. In the meantime, the group has been put up in a hotel suite with a television in the common room and a radio in each bedroom.

Altan, his curiosity piqued by something he caught on Occult Fan Radio, has gotten hold of a laptop and is tracking down the photo the hosts were discussing: a strange metallic fin found somewhere near Cornelia, captioned *"Cornelian Airship scraps? Or spaceship from the moon!? You decide!"* The comments underneath are split evenly between true believers and people insisting it is a car bumper.

He notices **Iomene** before she says anything. She has let herself into the room without knocking and is leaning over his shoulder to look at the screen, dressed in a well-tailored two-piece business suit with her hair up in a ponytail.

> [!dlg-guiscard-r] Guiscard
> "Bienvenue. Is anyone else going to appear in my room while I could be indecent?"

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "I thought we were in different rooms for a reason."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Oh, were you about to discuss 'boys' stuff'? I merely wanted to check on you, but in that case I will take my leave.
>
> ...But do you think the suit looks good on me? It's a memento from a job at a rather ambitious electric company."

Altan tells her she looks alright. Then, with the same casual tone, mentions that he noticed something while stabilizing her in the field. No pulse. Strangely positioned organs. He says he is not pushing it if she does not want to talk about it, but he thought she should know that he knows.

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
>> *lets out a small sigh*
>
> "Ahh well. He did tell me that any doctor worth their salt would reveal my little secret.
>
> Have you ever seen Gunblade Runner? The science fiction movie with the robots that weren't really robots?"

He hasn't.

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "For the record my name truly is Iomene Dervin, but my age is 497 years. Luthien is not my sister but my granddaughter.
>
> Nine years ago I contracted a serious illness that left me at death's door. My husband, unwilling to give up on me, put all his heart into the research of artificial life.
>
> I must admit I do not fully understand the true nature of this new body, but it truly isn't mechanical in nature. Zora described it as a kind of homunculus."

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "That makes sense. She does call you grandma."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
>> *puts a finger to her neck*
>
> "I never noticed that until now. How uncanny..."

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "I'm sorry for putting you on the spot, but maybe it let you get something off your chest."

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "It does feel somewhat liberating. Still, I'd like to ask you to keep this a secret."

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "Of course."

---

The Occult Fan Radio rerun plays on. The hosts cover a story about the Ring of King Solomon, a royal focus said to let the wielder call on ancient kings to fight at their side, and said to incinerate anyone unworthy who tries to wear it. Jay, the co-host, thinks the doom-train ring sounds cooler. Guiscard mentions, from the balcony, that he would trust either host about as far as he could throw them, and Jay is built like a bowling ball.

> [!dlg-iomene-r] Iomene
> "I have figured out that they're not exactly working with scholarly accepted sources.
>
> Hmm, I suppose nowadays it has become far easier to reach a bigger audience."

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "And that's part of the problem. We're going to be placed under heavy scrutiny after this. We've got to bump elbows with the elite and act like their knights in shining armor. Get your stories straight and don't deviate.
>
> We can't turn the capture of Garland into a conspiracy about an undead homunculus and a hunky alien, okay?"

> [!dlg-iomene-r] Iomene
> "I can attest to you that I am very much alive, unless you go by a radically different interpretation! My blood is red like yours and I have most of the organs you do. They just happen to be arranged a bit more... uniquely."

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "Okay, fine. A very much alive homunculus."

> [!dlg-altan-r] Altan
> "I've never thought my origin was a secret. Changing that now would be more of an inconsistency."

> [!dlg-guiscard] Guiscard
> "Not everyone at this royal get-together is going to like being saved by someone not even from this planet. Your brothers in arms could care less, but the Duke of Whatever is going to have some problems with it.
>
> I don't mean to sound disingenuous. I appreciate everything you bring to the table, and mon dieu am I worried about everything else."

> [!dlg-iomene-r] Iomene
> "The Dervin family hasn't been invited to such exclusive events for quite some time, but I can assure you I still know how to treat royalty and nobility.
>
> Do you two possess appropriate clothing for such an important event?"

Neither of them does.
Shopping, it is.

# The Morning After

Over breakfast, Luthien frames the matter with characteristic directness.

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "Now that everyone has shaken off yesterday's weariness, it is time for us to face a new crisis head-on. In other words: *we cannot show up at a grand ceremony as paupers.*"

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Oh, it is rare to see you express such interest in a social event! Could it be that you are on the search of a grandson-in-law for me?"

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "I merely believe we have the responsibility of representing the family in a good light. That is all."

They head to Amanoh Fine Silks. Inside, among a floor full of colors and patterns that would give any harlequin a field day, they find Audra already having her measurements taken. She waves. A tailor approaches. Luthien, uncharacteristically out of her element, glances at Iomene.

> [!dlg-iomene-r] Iomene
> "Hmm, as an elf you don't really have any shortcomings to cover, so we can fully concentrate on your strengths. I'd say colder colors naturally suit you."

Audra pulls the head tailor aside. They exchange a few quiet words. He looks at the four of them with a raised eyebrow. She gives a small, sheepish *...is that okay?* He goes to check on something.

What she arranged, it turns out, is a rare fabric. She offers to cover most of the cost.

> [!dlg-audra-r] Audra
> "You four have done wonders. Call it a thank you for everything."

# Ella's Wrath

They stop by Tiz and Elle's house on the way back. Someone has been busy with the graffiti again.

> [!narration]
> *"Onion Knight!"*
>
> *"Deserter!"*
>
> *"Die like Vyers did!"*

Luthien clears the wall with a wave of her hand before knocking.

Elle opens the door. Her happy expression lasts about half a second.

> [!dlg-elle-r] Elle
> "Tiz! Your *friends* who almost got you killed are here to see you again!"

She walks back into the kitchen without waiting for an answer, leaving the door open. Tiz pokes his head out from the table, waves, and invites them in just as a thin pan comes sailing out of the kitchen past his ear.

> [!dlg-elle-r] Elle
> "You know I didn't make nearly enough for six people!"

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "Okay, okay, sorry, honey!"

He gets to the door, voice dropping.

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "She's kinda mad about yesterday. I, um, told her everything.
>
> Tea is plenty enough for now. Come on in. Please."

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "Did you tell her you stood your ground valiantly?"

His smile tries to hold.

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "...I can't exactly say that with a straight face."

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "Are you kidding me, Tiz? Do you know what bravery means? It means feeling terror and fear, yet still overcoming it. That's what you did. There is no shame in what you felt. In fact it's precisely what makes your actions as commendable as they are."

Elle calls from the kitchen before he can answer. He heads in, still smiling, telling them to close the door on the way through.

He looks more energetic than the last time they were here.


---

The teapot is boiling. Elle is at the kitchen counter, chopping vegetables with the focused energy of someone working through feelings she has not yet decided to express. Tomatoes, celery, fennel. **Onions.**

> [!narration]
> Tiz sits nervously, occasionally looking at his wife, thinking of something to say to the rest of you.

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "Sssssso...
>
> I was invited to the grand banquet at the castle. Part of the celebration of the Princess returning."

> [!dlg-luthien-r] Luthien
> "Oh, that's splendid."
>> *whispers*
>
> "Will Elle be joining you?"

> [!dlg-iomene] Iomene
> "Ah, just like us then. You certainly deserve to be present as well."

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "We were both lucky to be invited."

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "No luck about it."

> [!dlg-elle] Elle
> "I'd call it less 'luck' and more a 'miracle' that we were *both* invited."

Tiz mentions his old suit still fits. Iomene tells him that simply means he has stayed in shape. He says it has been hard to find ways to stay active lately. Elle, still at the counter, does not look up.

> [!dlg-elle-r] Elle
> "Sneaking out of the house and running off to Cosmos know where is a great exercise trend, I hear."

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Wait, you snuck out?"

> [!dlg-elle-r] Elle
> "You can bet he did. The only thing I got was a note saying *'I hope to see you when I get back~ :)'*"

> [!dlg-luthien] Luthien
> "Tiz, Elle is your wife, not your mother. You can't just sneak out without telling her..."

> [!dlg-elle-r] Elle
> "How do you think I felt, thinking you wouldn't come back at all!?"

> [!narration]
> She lets out a sniveling whine, sniffling and turning around, in full tears now.

> [!dlg-elle] Elle
> "I was so scared you wouldn't come *back*...!"

> [!narration]
> The teapot is still boiling.

> [!dlg-tiz-r] Tiz
> "You're right.
>
> I'm really sorry. More than dying, I was scared I would've broken my promise to come back safe and sound to you.
>
> I promise I won't ever scare you again like that."

> [!dlg-elle] Elle
> "You better not...!"

Altan gets the kettle off the stove before it blows. Iomene watches the two of them with a quiet smile.

Once things have settled, Tiz mentions that he and Elle have been thinking seriously about Onrac. Fisherman's Horizon, or maybe the Blue District. Guiscard offers to set him up as his first franchisee in the arms business if he is looking for a new line of work. Iomene offers to arrange a meeting with Wedge Cunningway, and adds that she lives in the Blue District and can give them a tour.

> [!dlg-tiz] Tiz
> "I'm thinking of hanging up my helmet for good this time. Though I know someday I'm gonna need my sword."

Guiscard's flip phone rings. The tailor is ready for them. Before they can leave, Elle stops them at the door.

> [!dlg-elle-r] Elle
> "Sorry. For my attitude earlier. I've been a little... annoyed at my husband is all. I shouldn't have taken it out on you.
>
> Thank you for everything you've done for the both of us. No matter where we might end up going, you should know the four of you will always be welcome in our home."

On the way out, Iomene floats the idea of traveling to the banquet together. The graffiti on Tiz's wall has been replaced with fresh graffiti. Nobody comments on it.
